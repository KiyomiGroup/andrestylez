Project Overview

Swappy is a peer to peer trading platform designed to make it easy for people to exchange goods and services without money changing hands. The concept taps into something that has always existed informally, the idea of swapping what you have for what you need, and brings it into a structured, trustworthy digital space.

Nigeria has a deeply ingrained culture of trading and exchange. Markets, informal networks and WhatsApp groups already do a version of this every day. Swappy gives that behavior a proper home with a UI designed to make listings easy, browsing intuitive and communication between traders direct.

The Problem

Informal swapping and trading already happens constantly but with zero structure. People post in WhatsApp groups, get ignored, post again, get one reply, spend time negotiating with no clear format, and sometimes get scammed. The friction is real and it prevents the behavior from reaching its potential.

A proper platform solves this by giving both sides a structured way to list what they have, browse what they want, and initiate contact with a shared understanding of what the exchange involves.

User Flow

LISTING FLOW
User lands on homepage
  → Clicks "List an Item"
    → Fills in item name, condition, category
      → Describes what they want in exchange
        → Adds photo (optional)
          → Listing goes live

BROWSING FLOW
User lands on homepage
  → Browses listing categories
    → Clicks into a listing
      → Reads item details and desired swap
        → Clicks "I Have This" to propose exchange
          → Trader receives notification
            → They connect to arrange the swap

Trust and Safety Design

Trust signals were baked into the design from the start. Listings show how long an item has been posted, the category it belongs to, and a clear condition rating. These small details help browsers quickly assess whether a swap is worth pursuing. Nothing fancy, but everything deliberate.

The contact flow is designed to keep communication on platform as long as possible. Direct messages stay visible so both parties have a record of what was agreed, reducing the chance of misunderstanding or dispute.

Vibe Coding Process

Swappy was built using Claude as the primary coding partner. I described the functionality in detail and Claude generated the structure. I then refined it through iterative prompting, adjusting the visual design, tightening the layout and fixing edge cases as they appeared.

The marketplace listing logic, including filtering by category, sorting by recency and the swap proposal interface, was all generated through conversation with the AI. I reviewed, tested and shaped every piece, but the velocity was entirely different from traditional development.

Outcome

Swappy is live on GitHub Pages. It is a functional proof of concept for a peer to peer trading platform built entirely through vibe coding. The speed of execution and the quality of the result demonstrate what becomes possible when design thinking is combined with AI development tools.
